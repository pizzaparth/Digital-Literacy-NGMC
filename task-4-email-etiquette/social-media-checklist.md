# Checklist for Responsible Social Media Use

As a college student, your social media presence often serves as your "digital resume." Use this guide to ensure your online activity supports your personal and professional goals.

## The Do's

| Action | Why it Matters |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Audit your privacy settings** | Regularly check who can see your posts to ensure private moments stay private. |
| **Network with intention** | Follow industry leaders, professors, and companies you admire to stay informed. |
| **Showcase your projects** | Post about your hackathons, research, or volunteer work to build a portfolio. |
| **Verify before sharing** | Fact-check news or academic info to maintain your reputation for accuracy. |
| **Support your peers** | Use your platform to celebrate the achievements of classmates and colleagues. |

---

## The Don'ts

* **Don't post in the heat of the moment:** Avoid "venting" about professors, exams, or employers; digital footprints are permanent.
* **Don't overshare personal details:** Protect your safety by not posting your specific location, ID numbers, or daily schedule in real-time.
* **Don't ignore the "Grandmother Rule":** If you wouldn't want a grandparent or a future hiring manager to see it, don't post it.
* **Don't engage with trolls:** Avoid toxic arguments that can escalate and reflect poorly on your conflict-resolution skills.
* **Don't forget to untag:** If friends post photos of you that aren't professional, untag yourself or ask for them to be removed.
