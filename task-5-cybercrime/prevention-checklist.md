# Stay Safe Online: A Checklist for College Students in India

Navigating campus life and the digital world requires constant vigilance. Use this checklist to ensure your personal data, finances, and digital identity remain secure.

---

## Financial & UPI Safety

* **Never enter a PIN to receive money:** Remember that a UPI PIN is only required for sending money or checking your balance. If someone asks you to enter your PIN to "receive a refund" or "claim a prize," it is a scam.
* **Verify the Payee name before hitting send:** Scammers often use names similar to official organizations. Always double-check the verified name displayed on the UPI app before authorizing any transaction.
* **Avoid clicking payment links in SMS or WhatsApp:** Treat unsolicited links promising cashbacks or electricity bill updates as high-risk. Manually open your banking app instead of using external links.

---

## Account & Privacy Protection

* **Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA):** Activate 2FA on your university portals, email, and social media accounts. Use an authenticator app rather than SMS codes where possible to prevent SIM-swapping attacks.
* **Use unique passwords for every portal:** Avoid using your university ID or date of birth as a password. Use a password manager to maintain complex, unique strings for different services.
* **Audit social media privacy settings:** Restrict your profile visibility to "Friends Only" on platforms like Facebook and Instagram to prevent "identity cloning" or "doxxing" by strangers.

---

## Device & Network Security

* **Avoid Free Public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks:** Do not access banking apps or student portals over open cafeteria or station Wi-Fi. If necessary, use a trusted VPN or your mobile hotspot.
* **Disable Auto-Join for Wi-Fi and Bluetooth:** This prevents your device from connecting to malicious "honeypot" networks that may be set up near campus libraries or hangouts.
* **Keep software and OS updated:** Regularly update your smartphone and laptop OS to patch security vulnerabilities that hackers use to gain unauthorized access.

---

## Reporting Cybercrime

If you have been a victim of financial fraud, identity theft, or online harassment, report it immediately to the authorities.

* **National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal:** [https://cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
* **Cybercrime Helpline Number:** 1930 (Call immediately in case of financial fraud)
